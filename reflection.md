# Reflection

## Profile Comparisons

### Happy Pop Fan vs. Chill Lofi Listener
The Happy Pop Fan gets high energy upbeat tracks while the Chill Lofi Listener gets quiet atmospheric ones. Energy is the biggest difference between these two profiles and the genre labels point to completely separate parts of the catalog.

### High-Energy Rock vs. EDM Dance Floor
Both profiles want high energy but the genre split sends them in different directions. Rock vs EDM. The scores end up similar which shows energy is doing most of the work while genre handles the final sorting.

### Sad Indie Night vs. Happy Pop Fan
Flipping the mood from sad to happy changes the entire feel of the results. Mood acts as a tiebreaker once genre and energy have already narrowed things down.

## What I Learned

Recommender systems are only as good as their data. With 10 songs the system runs out of variety fast for niche profiles. Weights matter a lot too since doubling one feature can completely reorder the results. Simple systems can still feel smart but they break down quickly when preferences conflict or genres overlap. AI tools helped speed things up but every suggestion still needed to be checked carefully before using it.
